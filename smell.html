course
description

Over recent years, artists more and more are beginning to explore olfaction, or the sense of smell, as yet another way to engage humans in storytelling. What can gaining a deeper understanding of this oft used but not often thought about sense explain to us about the invisible forces that draw or repel us to connect with others and our overall experiences of being human?

The aim for this one-week intensive workshop is to learn as much as we can about olfaction, scents and designing smell experiences. We'll get hands-on with as many scent harvesting techniques (enfleurage, mixing scents, distilling) as possible while being inspired by scent artists active within the field. We'll also do some nose training and get into space design using scents. Additionally, this course coincides with our transmedia storytelling program. During the program, both groups will come together to experience what smell brings to the table as far as virtual and mixed reality experiences.

This course is for people who have  always wanted to delve into the world of olfaction but have  never been sure of where to begin. This one-week intensive will equip you with the technical expertise and confidence to continue  working on your own projects long after the course has ended.

who is this
program for?

This program is for artists, designers, technologists, researchers, and creative humans of all kinds interested in a deeper understanding of olfaction and learning how to create and design their own smell experiences for themselves and others.

This course approaches these topics from an introductory level. No prior experience is required.

Scent Club Berlin

Scent Club Berlin

Artistic Collective

Scent Club Berlin is an artistic collective and community of people that come together to research, produce projects and collaborate, focusing on the sense of smell. The group focuses on scent and olfaction as a medium for art, science, technology, expression and experimentation. Since its inception, the group has created interactive installations and tours in galleries and museums such as MMOMA Moscow, Hamburger Bahnhof, and Martin Gropius Bau in which the visitors are not only observers, but become participants in the work. The community of smell enthusiasts meets monthly and anyone interested in experimenting with the sense of smell is invited to join to share their knowledge and connect with other smell enthusiasts. The meetups range from lectures by biologists, artists and chemists, to distillations, scent experiments, discussions and more.
