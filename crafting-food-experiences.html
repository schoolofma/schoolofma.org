crafting food experiences

How can we design new experiences using food as material?

• 14. June - .18 June 2021
• Based at ACUD in Berlin, Germany
• One week, full-time
• Small class of participants

course
description

How can we change ours and others relationship to food through playful experimentation and storytelling? How can we craft new experiences around food, dining, and the experience of eating? Join us as we contemplate and craft new experiences around the stuff we put in our mouths.

This program is about crafting experiences using food as material. Over the course of five days we will be inspired by the works of a variety of brilliant food designers who will be joining us to share their practice. while also taking a hands-on approach to crafting unique experiences both individually and in groups. We will look at flavour and how the taste of food triggers memories and emotions, we'll consider aroma and chemical flavour compounds, ways of preparing food, and baking as a means of narrative storytelling.

The goal of this course is to be all-encompassing; we would love for participants to leave the course prepared to design and craft their own food experiences for friends, their communities, and the general public. Important is close attention to texture and the food itself, the ritual and timing of the experience, presentation, and the stories that can be drawn from these. This course coincides with our transmedia storytelling program. During the program, both groups will come together to experience what food brings to the table as far as virtual and mixed reality experiences.

This one-week intensive will equip you with the technical expertise and confidence to continue  working on your own projects long after the course has ended.

in this course,
you will be
introduced to

  • How to translate stories into food
  • How to create experimental food and dining experiences
  • Incorporating food into mixed reality experiences

  • An amazing network and community of like-minded creative beings and potential future collaborators

who is this
program for?

This program is for artists, designers, foodies, and any creative humans with an interest or passion for crafting unique and delightful food experiences in varying contexts that will be talked about long after they are over. This course approaches these topic from an introductory level, and yet aims to be all-encompassing, providing a rich and unique experience for all pwho attend.

Inés Lauber

Food Experience Designer

Inés Lauber designs and realizes (edible) room installations and events, always with the focus on food or based on the story around it. She create pop-up restaurants and bars, hosts workshops, arranges performances and guerilla-actions. Lauber works as a creative consultant for gastronomy, food producers and companies, experimenting with food and creating new recipes based on a healthy, modern lifestyles is something she is truly passionate about. She styles food and writes and talks about food and culture.

Since sustainability is fundamental to her work, she only uses ingredients of the best quality: organic, local, supportive of small business, preferably seasonal and, of course, fresh and tasty. Since 2013, Lauber is an active supporter of the Slow Food Movement in Berlin.

ineslauber.com/

Alexandra Genis

International Artist

Alexandra Genis is an international artist and the owner of TAS2R, a studio propagating Gastro-Intestinal Design-Fiction. She uses eating as a biochemical and visual tool to transmit ideas about ecology, innovation and science. Upon receiving her Bachelor of Arts from Design Academy Eindhoven, she exhibited in various locations such as Space 10 Copenhagen or BioSummit 3.0 at MIT Media Lab and has spoken at events, such as EMERGE conference, Minsk, or DDYA at Harvard GSD.

Alexandra is the Food, Design and Innovation correspondent at the Dutch Institute of Food and Design. She is based in Berlin.
