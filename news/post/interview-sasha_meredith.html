interview with sasha smirnova and meredith thomas

Two previous instructors, Sasha Smirnova, an artist with a background in architecture and urban research, and Meredith Thomas, an artist and creative technologist based in Berlin, are teaming up to teach If We Ruled The World II, a course focused on world-building, speculative futures, and 3D. deeper discussions about the hear and now and what a better world might look like! We interviewed them briefly for the blog in the run up to the course which begins this Thursday, 23. Seotember.


Rachel: Sasha, you’ve taught a version of this class with us before, what was that course about and how does this course differ?


Sasha: It wasn't the first time I ran this workshop, but it was the first time we had six weeks to think of potential and preferable future scenarios. The course exceeded my expectations in many senses: people's engagements, level of discussions, and topics. The goal of the workshop is not only to learn skills or participate in discussions but also to establish new contacts and bring the project and discussion in the "after-course" space, and this was exactly what happened after the course.

For "If We Ruled the World 2", we aim to bring the discussions around preferable futures into the 3D world that would add a visual layer to the speculative discussions around future societies. The goal is to build a representation of future scenarios that can take any shape from day-to-day interactions to new products or political organizations.



R: What can people gain from visualising possible future scenarios in 3D? What are some of your favourite examples of people doing so?


S: From the conceptual point of view, we will use 3D as a canvas to bring elements of the "future worlds" together and combine them into 3D collages. These collages would be representations of multiple futures we will imagine during the workshop. Participants work on the visualization from the very beginning of the course, adding elements according to their discussions around possible, probable, and preferable futures (‘Future Cone’ by Hancock and Bezoldand) gradually building the final collage.

From the technical points of view, we will be using existing 3D assets and bringing them into the 3D space in Unity. Those will be our artefacts from the future that will constitute the collage.

I would say Liam Young’s work is closely related to the idea of imagining futures based on the current state of the world.

Meredith: I think that giving artists the tools to better envisage alternative futures helps them to communicate those visions and shift the public imaginary. In digital form, these experiences, games or simulations can grow in complexity helping us to frame new narratives in engaging and unexpected ways.

Ian Cheng, Michael Frei and Sahej Rahal are three very different artists who spring to mind. Each succeeds in evoking not just an alternate, parallel or transcendent world within a game engine, but also in driving their narrative with satisfying and engaging simulations or interactions.



R: This course requires no prior knowledge. What skills is this course hoping to build up in someone?

M: In the first stages of learning a tool like Unity, I believe it is important to encourage a sense of freedom and playfulness. We will first learn the basics of importing and assembling 3D models and scenes as well as how to change their appearance with materials and lighting.

With this, you should already have the skills to start prototyping your visions. If we have time, we will move on to introducing interactivity with sounds and player interaction. This will only be a brief introduction to the software, but should serve as a jumping off point to further learning.

S: I believe that working on a concrete visual outcome will move the conversation to a different level. It will foster a discussion around strategies that can bring us to the preferable future and give tools to enhance analytical and critical skills of the participants.

Our goal is not to build dystopias, which is a relatively easy task, but rather think of future realities with their pros and cons and discuss what is “preferable” for us and how we could get there.



M: Sasha, what sources of material can people be considering for 3D collage?

S: The Internet is full of digital artefacts that sometimes are easy to find, and sometimes you need to dig deeper to get what you need. I imagine students bring any 3D models they can find online or build themselves into their worlds.

S: Meredith, from your teaching experience, do you think you could share an example of unusual approach in working with 3D that your students have used.

M: One past student and later collaborator, John Rogers built an AR app, with no prior experience during a class. The app served as a storytelling aid, for leading tours of an alternative future Galway. I really like how he used very simple techniques in combination with his other creative skills to build a transmedia experience that helped people visualise an alternative future in the place they lived.

R: You two have never worked together, what would you each say your strengths are and how will those strengths come together in this course?

M: I really enjoy teaching artists and creatives 3D software. It is always crazy satisfying to see the crazy diversity of things people choose to do with their new skills. I am hoping Sasha can help to further open our eyes to the possibilities in this space and help direct our efforts towards the difficult but essential challenge of imagining and finally, creating better worlds.

S: What excites me the most about the second edition of the course, is the 3D aspect of it. Besides the fact that students will learn new skills, I'm curious to see how abstract discussions will take shape and become a 3D visualization. I’m also interested in discovering collage techniques in 3D and seeing what happens when we bring found objects into one space. I see it as a sort of digital archaeology.

I’m sure Meredith's interest in teaching new technical skills will help students to unfold their ideas in very unexpected results.

If We Ruled the World II takes place 22. Sept - 27. Oct every Wednesday from 8-10pm CET. To reserve your spot visit: https://www.eventbrite.com/e/if-we-ruled-the-world-ii-tickets-166052494481
