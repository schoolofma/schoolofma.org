course
description

In this course, we will explore ex-centric & transhackfeminist methodologies, inhabit together a place in interference, bodily exorcisms, situated technology, techno-scientific wizardry, self-management of nomadic laboratories, critical anatomical illustrations. Additionally, we will share comparative historical tools and digital platforms; create, look and find low-cost technologies, share transfeminist biohacking methods and develop depatriarchalized history narratives to diagnosis and practices.

course outline

Week 1: Introductions, meet and share + Brief history of gynepunk

  • We will share personal context, why our experience bring us here, as a glimpse of the near future entanglements.
  • A brief presentation of the history of gynepunk, open to questions and feedback to connect and set the first personal/collective environment. (did you know the project before? Do you want to create a future/present with it?)

Week 2: History of medical disciplines + how to build Critical anatomies

  • Brief Annals of the infamous medical history: key events in OB/GYN, anatomy; proctology, urology; cosmetogynecology; genetics; neuroscience. We will discuss misoginoanatomy, trans-generational trauma, male hysteria. The context is mainstream medicine: racist, xenophobic, colonial, homophobic, transphobic, fatphobic, misogynist, and ableist status quo.
  • Body spells: GLAMS as recipes to transform and set out a different path of the historical references and parallel narratives. We will cook a seed water based lubricant to recovery flow of body storytelling, as the non-gendered prostate acknowledgment.

Week 3: Transhackfeminist (THF) genealogies and tools + disruptive developments and material manifestations

  • how THF! Becomes: events, practices and concept
  • Key references in Art: sculpture / performance / graphics / music / poetry /hacking
  • “Cuir Biology” inspirations as narrative inspirations.

Week 4: Medical health self-defense: decoding diagnosis, strategies resources & healing justice

  • hacking health discourses: restoring full responsibility in the assumption of one's disorders. 3Ds strategies: body DE-colonization, science DE-sacralization, civil DIS-obedience.
  • low cost techniques, an arsenal for visceral decolonization, recipes of DIY self defense: pepper spray, macerated inmunostimulants.
  • star up fermentation of next session: planning a proposal for “direct action”

Week 5: Direct action: visceral exorcisms and live movements

  • historical and contemporary examples of direct action organizations
  • Share and set a collective “guerrilla desire” organization of a direct strategy (speculative connections for disrupt reality based on medical experiences)

who is this
class for?

For everyone that has experienced medical violence. For everyone that has feel uncomfortable in a medical office, for those that health and mainstream medicine has only become suffering and disconnection. For health/medical students, health professionals, for all of “we” potential “patients”, all of us that wonder about ¿can this be different? ¿can we question the meaning of treatment, and health systems? ¿how we start and connect? For everyone that is open to talk and share this in a transhackfeminist based project.

What tools does the course aim to teach?

Share comparative historical tools and digital platforms; create, look and find low-cost technologies, share transfeminist biohacking methods and develop depatriarchalized history narratives to diagnosis and practices.

Martha Hipley is an artist and technologist with a journeyman’s approach to commercial digital products and conceptual art. She has exhibited work at Haus der elektronischen Künst, The Museum of Human Achievement, and Powrplnt, and received a microgrant from Rhizome for "untitled Twitter hack." She enjoys paintings and video games, and always wants to be making more of both.

She also writes and self-publishes An Artist's Guide to Computation, a newsletter of resources for creatives of all kinds who engage with technology.
